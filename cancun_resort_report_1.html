Resort 2 of 4 · Three Properties
Moon Palace Cancún

A 123-acre campus operating as three distinct properties sharing a coastline. Critical rule: Grand guests access all three sections freely. Nizuc and Sunrise guests cannot enter The Grand without paying $99/person. All sections include premium liquor, 24-hr room service, daily minibar, $750 resort credit (4-night stays), and airport transfers (4+ nights).

The Two-Section Model

The Reserve — Adults Only (18+)

Tower 5. Private pool, exclusive beach, restaurants La Palapa and Enso, Reserve Lounge with all-day cocktails and snacks, butler service via WhatsApp, guaranteed Bali beds, complimentary hydrotherapy spa, and one complimentary dinner at Sal Steak Cave.

Reviewers: "No screaming kids, no guests who've had a few too many." Ideal for the brothers and solo adults.

Family Concierge

Premium family wing with its own private pool, beach area, and dedicated lounge with all-day snacks, beverages, and a kids' candy buffet. Butler service, kid-size bathrobes, milk-and-cookies turndown, game console loans (PlayStation/Xbox/Wii), face painting, piñatas, and evening entertainment.

Both sections share 9 restaurants, beach, and new water park while maintaining distinct quiet zones.

Three dealbreakers: (1) Noise — Vegas-meets-Mexico vibe; DEZ nightclub runs until sunrise; Travel Weekly: "If quiet and understated is what you're after, look elsewhere." Reviews explicitly warn against this resort for families with infants needing sleep. (2) Poor beach — chronic seaweed, water described as brown and murky; attendants advise against swimming. Planning Notes
November Travel Tips
🌤️

Weather

84°F highs, 71°F lows. Mostly sunny. Transition to dry season. Occasional cold fronts may bring 1–2 windy days.

🌿

Seaweed

Sargassum is minimal November–March. Hyatt Ziva peninsula is most protected. Always verify before traveling.

🛡️

Safety

Quintana Roo is U.S. State Dept Level 2 — same rating as France and the UK. No visa required. Travel insurance recommended.

🚗

Transfers

Budget $60–$150 per group unless included. Moon Palace and AVA include transfers. Hyatt Ziva: ~$165 RT private SUV.

🧴

Pack Smart

Reef-safe sunscreen, swim diapers, OTC medicines, preferred baby food. Resort gift shops charge 3–4× retail.

🍼

Baby Food

Not provided at any resort. Supermarket within 5 min of Hyatt Ziva. Confirm cribs at booking with all properties.

🎭

Timeshares

Every resort uses timeshare reps. A firm "no thank you" at check-in is all that's needed. Never attend a "welcome orientation."

💳

Hyatt Points

~40,000 pts/night standard at Hyatt Ziva. Hybrid points + cash on 1–2 rooms can meaningfully offset group costs.

Bottom Line
Final Recommendations

For the Families

Best Option

Paradisus Cancún — Family Concierge Suite at ~$450–$575/night. Purpose-built layout with a privacy door separating the living area (crib-ready) from the king bedroom, Jacuzzi on the terrace, and full FC butler service. Everything under 8 months old. For the most dramatic suite, the Hyatt Ziva Pyramid Suite ($800–$1,200/night) delivers a breathtaking two-story layout — book immediately as only a handful exist.

For the Brothers & Solo Adults

Best Retreat Options

At Paradisus: The Reserve Adults-Only section — private pool, exclusive La Palapa and Enso restaurants, butler via WhatsApp, hydrotherapy spa. Best adults-only setup of any resort reviewed.

At Hyatt Ziva: Turquoize Tower — rooftop infinity pool, private hot tub, exclusive La Bastille French dining.

At Moon Palace Grand: Two adults-only pools and a Speakeasy bar.

For Budget-Conscious Members

Value Picks

Moon Palace Nizuc — Superior Family Deluxe at ~$400–$500/night with connecting rooms. $750 resort credit offsets extras, transfers included, 24-hr café is perfect for babies on irregular schedules. Free access to Sunrise's toddler splash park.

Paradisus Standard Junior Suite ($295–$375/night) is the lowest entry point at a freshly renovated property.

🏆 Group Verdict: Paradisus Cancún

Paradisus uniquely solves this group's core challenge: families need kid infrastructure and quiet; adults want tranquility and a proper retreat. The Reserve / Family Concierge split lets both coexist under one roof without compromise. The $50M renovation means brand-new everything at shoulder-season pricing. Butler service handles dining logistics for the full group. The November timing — minimal seaweed, pleasant weather, moderate crowds — aligns perfectly.

Runner-up: Hyatt Ziva Cancún — edges ahead on service consistency (Top 1% worldwide), food quality, and beach quality. The ideal alternative if the group can secure Pyramid Suites for the families, or for any Hyatt points holders looking to offset costs.

Remove from consideration: AVA Resort. Despite exceptional food and stunning modern design, the noise levels, poor beach, and airplane proximity make it the wrong fit for this group.
